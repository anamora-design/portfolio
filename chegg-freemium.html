Introduction

Background

Chegg is a popular subscription-based homework help platform for 8+ million college students worldwide.

Due to a massive need for online education during the pandemic, there was a huge influx of traffic to Chegg. While daily unique visitors increased, the conversion rate remained stagnant. The business wanted to convert more of these visitors into paying customers. The Growth & Monetization org hypothesized that reducing friction in the lower funnel would help with conversion, which became a key focus.

Problem to be solved

Chegg’s funnel encouraged account creation, but many users would end up not converting to a paid subscriber. The signed-in experience was generally geared toward paid subscribers who would have full access to content.

Although our customers prefer free trials, this was not a lever we could pull. What else could we do after creating an account to better serve these users and warm them up to converting?

Design Process

Research and Data

User Research

Comprehensive user research was conducted to understand the target audience and their needs. This included user interviews, surveys, and behavioral analysis to identify key insights and opportunities for improvement.

The research revealed several important findings about user behavior, preferences, and pain points that would inform the design decisions throughout the project.

Key insights from user research that informed design decisions.

Competitive Analysis

A thorough analysis of competitors and market leaders was conducted to understand industry standards, best practices, and opportunities for differentiation.

Design Hypotheses

Based on the research findings, three key hypotheses were developed to guide the design process and experimentation.

Hypothesis #1

Improving the visual hierarchy will increase user comprehension and task completion rates.

Hypothesis #2

Streamlining the user flow will reduce friction and improve conversion rates.

Hypothesis #3

Adding personalized elements will increase user engagement and retention.

Outcomes

Final Design Solution

The final design solution successfully addressed the identified problems and achieved the project goals. The new design improved user experience, increased engagement, and met all accessibility requirements.

Key Learnings

  • User research is crucial for understanding real user needs and pain points.
  • Iterative design and testing leads to better outcomes than assumptions alone.
  • Cross-functional collaboration is essential for successful product design.
  • Accessibility should be considered from the beginning of the design process.
